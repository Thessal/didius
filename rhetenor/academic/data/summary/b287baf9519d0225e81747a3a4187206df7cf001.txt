On March 9 2020 the VIX rose to 62.12, the highest level since the 2008 financial crisis, driven by the 2020 Russia–Saudi Arabia oil price war and the COVID‑19 pandemic.  
On March 12 2020 the VIX hit and closed at 75.47, surpassing the previous Black Monday value, after a travel ban to the United States from Europe was announced by President Trump.  
On March 16 2020 the VIX closed at 82.69, the highest level since its inception in 1990.  
The U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission fined S&P Dow Jones Indices for halting data on February 5 2018.  
VIX is sometimes criticized as a prediction of future volatility, but it is more accurately described as a measure of the current price of index options.  
VIX has virtually the same predictive power as past volatility, as indicated by nearly identical correlation coefficients in linear regressions.  
Volatility in the VIX chart is measured as the standard deviation of S&P 500 one‑day returns over a month‑long period.
