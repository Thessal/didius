On March 9, 2020, the VIX hit 62.12, the highest level since the 2008 financial crisis.  
On March 12, 2020, the VIX hit and closed at 75.47, exceeding the previous Black Monday value.  
On March 16, 2020, the VIX closed at 82.69, the highest level since its inception in 1990.  
VIX is sometimes criticized as a prediction of future volatility, but it is described as a measure of the current price of index options.  
Volatility is measured as the standard deviation of S&P 500 one‑day returns over a month’s period.  
The VIX has virtually the same predictive power as past volatility, as indicated by nearly identical correlation coefficients in linear regressions.  
The U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission fined S&P Dow Jones Indices for halting data on February 5, 2018.
