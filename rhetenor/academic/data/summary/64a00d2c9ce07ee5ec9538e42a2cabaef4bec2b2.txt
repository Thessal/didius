When a five-wave rally completes within a channel on a linear scale, there is no breakout.  
When the pattern is completed by wave 5 touching the parallel line on a logarithmic chart, there will be no further price advance until the price breaks the baseline.  
The Dow Index (DJIA) has followed the same five-wave pattern for almost the same period as patent filings from 1850 to 1942.  
Stocks followed the same pattern during the same period, except that from 1928 to 1942 the correction was a triangle instead of waves A, B, and C.
