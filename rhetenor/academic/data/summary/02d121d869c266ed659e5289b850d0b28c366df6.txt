Morgan coined the name "McClellan Oscillator" for the indicator created by the McClellans, and it became a daily feature on his TV show.  
The McClellan Oscillator and Summation Index gained appreciation among technically inclined investors after exposure on KWHY-TV and seminars, spreading across the United States.  
The McClellan Oscillator and Summation Index gained a wider following in the 1980s when personal computers allowed early technical analysis programs like Computrac to include them.  
The McClellans updated their book in 1989 to note that calculations of the McClellan Oscillator could now be done with personal computers.  
Divergences between the McClellan Oscillator and price action can signal an impending change in price direction.  
The McClellan Oscillator functions as an accelerometer for market breadth statistics.  
Measuring acceleration of the McClellan Oscillator can help signal an impending trend change.  
Divergences between the McClellan Oscillator and price action occur more often at market tops than at bottoms.  
A congestion zone is a sustained period where the McClellan Oscillator fluctuates by very small increments over several days, typically forming above the zero line.  
Congestion zones of the McClellan Oscillator are rarely observed at extended negative values.
