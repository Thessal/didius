Morgan coined the name "McClellan Oscillator" for the indicator created by the McClellans, and it became a daily feature on his TV show.  
The McClellan Oscillator and Summation Index gained appreciation among technically inclined investors in Los Angeles after exposure on KWHY-TV and seminars, and spread across the United States.  
The McClellan Oscillator and Summation Index gained a wider following in the 1980s after personal computers enabled early technical analysis programs like Computrac to include them.  
By 1989 the McClellans updated their book to note that calculations of the Oscillator could now be done with personal computers.

The McClellan Oscillator functions as an accelerometer for market breadth statistics.  
Measuring acceleration of the Oscillator can help signal an impending change in trend direction.  
Divergences between the Oscillator and price action occur more often at market tops than at bottoms, reflecting the typical shape of US stock market tops and bottoms.  
A congestion zone is a sustained period during which the Oscillator fluctuates by very small increments over several days.  
Congestion zones of the McClellan Oscillator usually form above the zero line and are rarely seen at extended negative values.
