VIX has virtually the same predictive power as past volatility, as shown by nearly identical correlation coefficients.  
VIX is sometimes criticized as a prediction of future volatility, but it is described as a measure of the current price of index options.  
Critics claim that despite sophisticated formulation, the predictive power of most volatility forecasting models is similar to that of plain‑vanilla measures such as simple past volatility.  
Other works have countered that these critiques failed to correctly implement the more complicated volatility models.  
The risk inherent in attempting to time short‑term volatility is often overlooked.  
Some practitioners and portfolio managers have questioned the depth of understanding of the fundamental concept of volatility.  
Daniel Goldstein and Nassim Taleb titled a research article “We Don’t Quite Know What We Are Talking About When We Talk About Volatility.”  
Emanuel Derman has expressed disillusion with empirical models that are unsupported by theory.
